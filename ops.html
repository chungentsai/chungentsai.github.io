Online Portfolio Selection (OPS)

OPS is a multi-round game between two players, say Investor and Market. Let \(d\) be the number of investment alternatives and \(T\) be the number of rounds.

At the \(t\)-th round,

  1. Investor chooses a portfolio \(x_t\in\Delta_d\subseteq\mathbb{R}^d\).

  2. Market announces a vector of price relatives \(r_t\in[0,\infty)^d\).

  3. Investor suffers a loss \(f_t(x_t) := -\log\langle r_t,x_t\rangle\).

The regret is defined as \(R_T := \sum_{t=1}^T f_t(x_t) - \min_{x\in\Delta_d} \sum_{t=1}^T f_t(x)\).

Current Efficiency-Regret Pareto Frontier

 

The first paper using the term “Pareto frontier” in OPS is the work of Zimmert et al. (2022).

Existing OPS Algorithms

The minimax-optimal regret is \(\Theta(d\log T)\) (Ordentlich and Cover (1998)).
